Services · Test Preparation

Small cohorts. Senior coaches. Real score gains.

No hundred-student auditoriums. Our online test-prep runs in cohorts of 6–10 led by experienced coaches who track every student's band-by-band progression.

Book free consultation →See the process
Who it's for

Built for Any UAE or GCC student or professional preparing for a standardised test.

We run regular cohorts for IELTS, TOEFL, GRE, GMAT, SAT, UCAT, LNAT and BMAT. Classes run in the evenings (GST) and on weekends to fit around school and work schedules.

Tests we coach

Eight tests. Across every level.

IELTS (Academic + General) · TOEFL iBT · GRE · GMAT (and GMAT Focus Edition) · SAT · UCAT · LNAT · BMAT-replacement tests. We don't coach CAT, JEE or local UAE entrance tests.

6–10 Students per cohort
+1.0 Average IELTS band gain
+60 Average TOEFL iBT score gain
Diagnostics

Diagnostics, then targeted skill-building.

Every student starts with a full diagnostic test. We map weak sub-skills (e.g. "paraphrasing in IELTS Writing Task 2" rather than "writing"), then build a 6–10 week plan with targeted homework.

Mock tests & analytics

Four full mocks per cohort.

Full-length, timed, scored — with granular question-type analytics so you know exactly where to focus your final push.

One-on-one support

Small cohort + office hours.

Every cohort includes weekly office-hour slots for one-on-one help. No extra fee.

How it works

Our Test Preparation process.

Free diagnostic test

Sit a full-length diagnostic in our test centre or online. We score it and walk you through every weak spot.

Personalised study plan

6–10 week plan with weekly homework and check-ins, tailored to your diagnostic gaps.

Live cohort classes

2 sessions per week of 90 minutes each, via Zoom. Recorded for later review.

Weekly office hours

One-on-one slots for targeted help.

Full-length mock tests

4 mocks spaced through the programme, scored with analytics breakdowns.

Test-day strategy session

A final session 3–5 days before your test to lock in mental strategy, timing rules, and red-flag avoidance.
