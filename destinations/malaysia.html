Application process

Your path to Malaysia, in six moves.

Decide: Malaysian, branch campus or private

Three categories: public (UM, USM), international branches (Monash, Nottingham, Heriot-Watt), private (Taylor's, Sunway). Each has different fee structures and degree issuance.

Take IELTS / TOEFL / MUET

IELTS 6.0 is the common benchmark; MUET accepted by public universities. Several private universities accept UAE English-medium school-leaving scores in lieu.

Apply direct to each university

No central application. Deadlines typically 2–3 months before each intake (Jan, May, Sept).

Receive offer & single entry visa

EMGS (Education Malaysia Global Services) processes all student applications centrally. Takes 2–4 weeks. Single entry visa issued on approval.

Arrive, convert to multi-entry student pass

On arrival, medical check + thumb-print + multi-entry student pass valid 1 year, renewed annually.

Twin-degree transfer (optional)

Monash Malaysia, Nottingham Malaysia and Heriot-Watt Malaysia all allow 1–2 years in Malaysia followed by transfer to UK/AUS main campuses — at significant savings.

Visa & post-study work

The Malaysia visa route in plain English.

Student Pass

Study

Student Pass. Single entry for visa + multi-entry after arrival, valid 1 year, annually renewable. Part-time work up to 20 hours/week during holidays only.

Employment Pass

Post-Study

Post-study Employment Pass. Graduates can apply for Employment Pass with a job offer. Minimum monthly salary thresholds apply (MYR 5,000–10,000 depending on sector).

DE Rantau Nomad Pass

Digital Nomad

DE Rantau Nomad Pass. For remote workers with foreign employers — 12 months renewable. Useful for some graduates.

MM2H Residence

Long-term

MM2H (long-term residence). Not a student route but a longer-term option some families consider. Income and deposit requirements were revised in 2024.

Living & costs

What it actually costs to live here.

Monthly Budget

Finance

Monthly budget. Kuala Lumpur MYR 1,500–3,000 (~USD 330–660); Penang/JB MYR 1,200–2,200; smaller cities MYR 1,000–1,800. The lowest cost-of-living among serious study destinations.

Accommodation

Housing

Accommodation. On-campus housing MYR 500–1,200/month; private condos MYR 1,000–2,500/month (shared MYR 500–1,000). Quality of private accommodation is high.

Food & Transport

Affordable

Food & transport. Food is famously cheap and excellent — MYR 10–25/meal at hawker stalls. Grab (ride-sharing) is affordable. KL has LRT/MRT.

Tropical Climate

Climate

Climate. Tropical — hot and humid year-round with afternoon rain. Expect to run AC continuously. Factor this into accommodation budgets.

Scholarships & funding

The funded routes into Malaysia.

Monash Merit

Up to 50%

Monash Merit & Leadership. Monash Malaysia offers up to 50% tuition reduction for strong applicants. Leadership and equity scholarships available in addition.

Nottingham Award

High Achievers

Nottingham High Achievers Award. Automatic consideration for top applicants — up to 20% tuition reduction on Bachelor's programmes.

Taylor's Excellence

Up to 100%

Taylor's Excellence Awards. Up to 100% tuition for outstanding school-leaving results. Specific band cut-offs published annually.

Gov Scholarships

Limited

Government scholarships (limited for UAE). MIS (Malaysian International Scholarship) is primarily for Muslim-majority nation students and some other country lists. UAE nationals check eligibility in-year.
